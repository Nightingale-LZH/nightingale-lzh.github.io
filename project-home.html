Me Getting Wild

I have a deep passion for mechanical engineering and computer science. My studies in computer engineering, specializing in embedded systems, gave me the foundational skills to explore my love for tinkering and problem-solving. During my undergraduate years, I spent considerable time in engineering clubs, where I honed my skills. I was certified in manual mill and lathe operation and gained experience in manufacturing composite materials like carbon fiber and glass fiber.

Nowadays, I practice craftsmanship and engineering purely for fun. Especially, I love to overengineer solutions to everyday problems. My workflow is heavily centered around 3D printing. Pen making is a major part of my weekend hobby, driven by my love for fountain pens and the joy of writing with them. In addition to hands-on projects, I pursue passion-driven electronics and programming endeavors, including creating a macro keyboard and a finite-state machine compiler.
