Aeolia A2 CusM63

Rollerball, (March 2024)

This is a rollerball pen with unibody design and optimizes for tripod grip. It uses Lamy M63 rollerball refill. The entire pen barrel is solid one-piece, hence unibody, without any separations. However, the friction-fitted pen tip retaining the refill often pops out under high writing pressure. This problem has been fixed in Aeolia A10.

Aeolia A3 CusVanishing

The Gold-Nib Fountain Pen, (April 2024)

This is a fountain pen has the same classic silhouette as other Aeolia pens. It is based on the Pilot Vanishing Point inner pen, which has gold-nib option under $100 (at least in 2024). It has a gap-free smooth barrel with tripod grip indents.

Aeolia A5 CusStyleFit

Mechanical Pencil, (May 2024)

This is a grip-advance, long-nosed mechanical pencil that uses Mitsubishi Pencil's Uni-Stylefit core. The lead is advanced by pressing up the grip section, so users can maintain their gripping posture. The grip section can be removed to reveal the long and thin barrel that is ideal for precision marking. This is often used for carpentry and other manufacturing work.

Aeolia A6 CusSF-Duo

Double-Nib Mechanical Pencil, (May 2024)

This is double-nib mechanical pencil, design for engineers who need to use multiple colored pencil. The user can swap to other nib by rotating the pen, (or use two nib simultaneously). The two pencils can advance lead separately. The regular version has same footprint as Aeolia A3 and the compact version is 20% shorter than regular pens.

Aeolia A7 CusVP

Retractable Fountain Pen, (June 2024)

One of my BEST creation

It is based on the Pilot Vanishing Point inner pen. It is retractable and a sliding cover that seal the pen when retracted. The design process of the pen body, unlike other pens, is similar to sculpture, where all the facets are subtracted away and all the beautiful lines remained. It is my best design so far, but too complicated for anyone without extensive knowledge about this pen to use.
